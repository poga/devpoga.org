Reading it feels like parsing raw METAR codes or shipping manifests. Bots exchange JSON while humans abandon the medium. The result is perfectly efficient and completely alienating. Humans visiting websites now feel like trespassers in a factory. The aesthetic is pure machine readability. ### LLM-powered propaganda defeats every defense AI-generated disinformation spreads faster than humans can counter it. Tools like Polis and Agora Citizen function as digital Maginot Lines—elaborate but circumvented. The poison outruns the antidote. Communities collapse faster than they can adapt. ### Consensus reality ends When thousands of equally polished "truths" exist, shared reality disappears. Every historical consensus mechanism depended on production bottlenecks. Those bottlenecks are gone. We cannot agree on what happened yesterday. Parallel realities have become literal, not metaphorical. ### Communities shrink to pre-digital trust circles Trust networks contract to pre-digital size while maintaining digital walls. Discord servers operate as speakeasies with secret handshakes. Communities invent authentication rituals: "Send a photo of your desk right now." Human CAPTCHAs return. So do letters of introduction. ### Physical proof replaces digital claims Physical presence and tangible output become the only credible signals. "I met them in person" replaces the blue check. Local impact becomes the only verifiable change. ### Pull requests lose their proof-of-work Creating a pull request used to signal competence. Writing code that passes tests and looks legitimate required effort. That effort built trust. Now open source drowns in AI-generated PRs. The barrier is gone. Maintainers spend more time filtering garbage than reviewing contributions. ### Globalization reverses as the world fragments Shared beliefs fracture along predictable fault lines. Machine translation made language irrelevant just as cultural walls became impermeable. International collaborations fail. "Extremely online" no longer describes a universal culture. Digital tribalism reinforces geographic borders. ### Impact shrinks to local scale Activism moves offline. "Hyperlocal" replaces "viral." Presence carries political weight. New leverage builds from accumulated local actions. We must find impact metrics that ignore reach. --- ### Review **The central problem:** AI destroyed the production bottlenecks that created credibility. We can no longer distinguish truth from fabrication at scale. The internet optimizes for machine readability. Humans become edge cases. Weather satellite data captures this precisely—functional infrastructure nobody wants to use. Bots exchange JSON while humans evacuate. LLM-generated content floods every defense mechanism faster than humans can respond. Polis, Agora Citizen, and similar tools fail because they solve yesterday's problem. They assumed human-speed production. That assumption broke. The collapse of consensus reality follows directly. Every historical agreement mechanism relied on production limits. Those limits vanished. We cannot establish shared facts when thousands of equally polished narratives exist simultaneously. Communities contract to pre-digital trust circles while maintaining digital walls. Discord servers require human CAPTCHAs. "Send a photo of your desk right now" becomes authentication. Physical presence becomes the only credible signal because digital signals became worthless. The pull request example hits hardest. Open source relied on effort as filter. Submitting working code proved understanding. That proof means nothing now. Maintainers shift from collaborators to gatekeepers. The contribution model breaks entirely. Globalization reverses at the exact moment machine translation eliminated language barriers. Cultural walls solidify as communication friction disappears. International collaboration fails not from inability to communicate, but from inability to trust. The conclusion—"impact metrics without reach"—states the requirement but leaves the method unclear.
